⚡ Quick Summary

  • ASMR-Bench introduces a benchmark for detecting sabotage in autonomous ML research codebases — a critical trust infrastructure problem as AI begins conducting its own experiments.
  • Task Rewards vs Distribution Sharpening shows that RL training genuinely instills new skills in frontier models rather than just sharpening existing capabilities — a key debate in LLM alignment.
  • Gradient Fingerprints detects reward hacking in RLVR by identifying implicit CoT exploits, offering a scalable audit mechanism for verifiable-reward training pipelines.
  • Weber's Conjecture proven for k≤12 unconditionally (no GRH assumption), closing a 140-year open problem in module lattice cryptography with direct implications for MLWE security proofs.
  • Fast dual-rail qubit erasure detection enables hardware-efficient quantum error correction at arbitrary circuit positions, a key step toward fault-tolerant quantum computing.

  • AI Agent Build: A first-time coder shared their experience building a 22k-line application using AI coding agents, revealing the challenges of controlling agent behavior and managing long-horizon workflows.
  • NN Vulnerability: Flipping just 2 sign bits in a neural network's weights can reduce ResNet-50 accuracy by 99.8%, exposing a new attack surface in deep learning models.
  • AI Alignment: A systems engineering perspective reviews alignment, agency, and autonomy in frontier AI, highlighting the lack of universal definitions across disciplines and its implications for governance.
