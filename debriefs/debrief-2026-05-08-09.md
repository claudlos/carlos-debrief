## Quick Summary

- <strong>UniPool</strong> introduces a globally shared expert pool for MoE architectures, decoupling depth scaling from expert-parameter growth and improving validation loss across all model scales.
- <strong>Balanced Aggregation (BA)</strong> resolves a fundamental tension in GRPO-style RLVR between token-level and sequence-level gradient aggregation, yielding consistent gains on math and coding benchmarks.
- <strong>SkillOS</strong> enables LLM agents to self-evolve by learning to curate reusable skills from accumulated experience via a closed RL loop, outperforming memory-based baselines.
- <strong>Continuous Latent Diffusion Language Model (Cola DLM)</strong> proposes hierarchical latent diffusion as a non-autoregressive alternative to autoregressive LLMs, achieving strong scaling behaviour up to ~2000 EFLOPs.
- <strong>XL-SafetyBench</strong> reveals that jailbreak robustness and cultural awareness are uncoupled in frontier LLMs, and that many local models' apparent safety is simply generation failure rather than genuine alignment.

## News Headlines

- <strong>Firefox hardens against AI-generated bugs</strong>: Mozilla is using Claude-powered agentic harnesses to automatically reproduce and triage real browser bugs, shifting the economics of automated security.
- <strong>ChatGPT/Cloudflare React-state snooping exposed</strong>: A developer decrypted the mechanism by which Cloudflare reads client-side React state before ChatGPT accepts input, raising fresh questions about SaaS privacy boundaries.
- <strong>Cross-cultural LLM safety gaps surface</strong>: XL-SafetyBench finds most frontier models' safety scores obscure dramatic variation across country-language pairs, with local models often failing for the wrong reasons.
- <strong>RL training instability in GRPO reliably fixed</strong>: The Balanced Aggregation paper provides a simple drop-in replacement for GRPO's core aggregation step, resolving a systematic bias affecting every major reasoning-model training run.
- <strong>Shared expert pools challenge per-layer MoE orthodoxy</strong>: Analysis showing that top-k router replacement drops accuracy by only ~1 point across production MoE models is prompting rethink of the rigid per-layer expert allocation rule.
