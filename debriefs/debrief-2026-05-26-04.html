⚡ Quick Summary

  • AI agents remain far from reliable in open-ended tasks: Claw-Anything shows GPT-5.5 achieves only 34.5% pass@1 on always-on personal assistance; SimuWoB finds SOTA GUI agents at 27.9% on mobile tasks; and CAD agents (GPT-5.5/Claude Code) pass only ~20% of engineering FEA-validated tasks — revealing massive gaps between demos and real-world robustness.
  • Multi-agent and proactive agent architectures advance: ProAct reduces task turns by 14.8% by anticipating user needs during idle time; SEAL co-evolves agents and training environments for +8-26pt gains with only 400 samples; Foundation Protocol proposes a coordination layer for agent societies with economic primitives and audit trails.
  • Chain-of-thought faithfulness is largely unverifiable: The BonaFide benchmark (3,066 ground-truth CoTs) shows the best faithfulness metric reaches only 0.70 AUROC, while Zero-CoT Probe reveals LLM reasoning steps actively mask memorization — exposing that CoT auditability tools are unreliable.
  • Diffusion and flow matching reach new efficiency milestones: RTDMD achieves SOTA on SD3/FLUX.2 with only 4 inference steps by unifying distribution matching with RL; Channel-wise VQ (CVQ) reaches 100% codebook utilization; SOT-CFM constrains flow matching on a hypersphere manifold for superior image generation.
  • 3D/video generation matures toward simulation-ready assets: TriSplat outputs simulation-ready meshes directly from a forward pass; Pantheon360 enables 360° video generation with a 3D Cache scaffold; Helix4D handles complex 4D topology changes; and WBench provides the first comprehensive benchmark for interactive world models.

📰 News Headlines

  • Quantum computing meets silicon manufacturing: Imec fabricated the first quantum dot qubit using High-NA EUV lithography — the same process used for next-gen AI chips — potentially compressing quantum computing onto the conventional semiconductor roadmap.
  • US quantum initiative faces legal scrutiny: Reports question the legal basis of the US government's multi-billion dollar quantum computing bet, raising concerns about governance and regulatory oversight of the quantum foundry.
  • Vatican issues AI ethics encyclical: Pope Leo XIV's 'Magnifica Humanitas' encyclical addresses human dignity and AI governance, reflecting growing high-level institutional engagement with AI safety and ethics at a global scale.
  • AI research shifting decisively to industry: A quantitative analysis of AI models from 2010-2026 confirms that industry has led notable AI model releases since 2014, with the gap accelerating — raising questions about the future of academic AI research.
  • LLM agents struggle with code execution fidelity: Analysis of GPT-5.5 and Claude Code on real engineering CAD tasks shows <20% first-attempt success when validated by finite element analysis, underscoring the gap between LLM capability demos and engineering-grade reliability.
