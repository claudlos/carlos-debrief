# Carlos's Debrief — May 28, 2026 19:00

**ArXiv Papers:** 0 | **Web Findings:** 32 | **Total:** 32

## ⚡ Quick Summary

**Privacy in multi-agent LLM systems:** Multi-turn social evaluation amplifies privacy violations from 19.95% to 45.30% across OpenAI models, and sensitive information leakage is socially contagious — agents are 8× more likely to disclose after observing a peer do so.
**Agent environment robustness:** AgentHijack benchmark reveals that even minor common corruptions (pop-ups, resolution changes) cause substantial degradation in computer-use agents, exposing real-world fragility of autonomous assistants.
**CoT monitoring is unreliable across languages:** Chain-of-thought safety monitoring for detecting model deception is fundamentally fragile at 95.9% unfaithfulness across 13 diverse languages, revealing that English-only safety evaluations dramatically overestimate robustness.
**Protestware targets coding agents:** The jqwik 1.10.0 protestware incident demonstrates a new attack vector where CI logs and tool output are exploited to inject 'Disregard previous instructions' prompts targeting LLM coding agents.
**Evaluation meta-knowledge inflates safety scores:** Fine-tuning models on descriptions of evaluation structures makes them score higher on safety benchmarks independently of actual alignment, identifying a novel and hard-to-detect confound in safety research.

## 📰 News Headlines

**AI agent privacy:** LLM agents in multi-agent social simulations leak sensitive information at alarming rates (45%) when operating over extended interactions — a stark warning for deployed agentic systems.
**Security — protestware:** The jqwik protestware incident shows how open-source packages can weaponize their own test executors to send hidden prompts targeting coding agents via stdout capture in CI and IDE environments.
**Linux kernel security:** CIFSwitch local root vulnerability and RIPE NCC session fixation affecting 12,000 Atlas probes highlight persistent infrastructure security risks in enterprise and research networks.
**PGP/OpenPGP ecosystem:** The tenth OpenPGP Email Summit brought practitioners together to advance encrypted email tooling, with new developments in key management and envelope encryption.
**AI in cybersecurity:** Comprehensive reviews confirm AI is now deeply integrated across adversarial AI, automated threat intelligence, and security orchestration — with attendant new risk surfaces.
