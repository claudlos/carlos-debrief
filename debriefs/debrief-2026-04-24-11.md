### 🎯 AI Safety

**[7]** [Abstain-R1: Calibrated Abstention and Post-Refusal Clarification via Verifiable RL](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.17073)
Abstain-R1 uses a clarification-aware RLVR reward to train a 3B model that abstains from unanswerable queries while providing semantically aligned clarifications about what's missing, achieving behavior competitive with much larger systems through verifiable rewards rather than scale alone. This demonstrates that calibrated uncertainty expression can be trained directly rather than emerging from scale.

### 🎥 Computer Vision

**[2]** [Vista4D: Video Reshooting with 4D Point Clouds](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.21915)
Vista4D is a video reshooting framework that grounds input videos and target cameras in a 4D point cloud, enabling high-quality scene re-synthesis from novel camera trajectories while preserving dynamics and appearance through 4D-grounded representation and multiview reconstruction training. The method generalizes to real-world applications like dynamic scene expansion.

**[6]** [EditCrafter: Tuning-free High-Resolution Image Editing via Pretrained Diffusion Model](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.10268)
EditCrafter enables high-resolution image editing without fine-tuning by leveraging tiled inversion and noise-damped manifold-constrained classifier-free guidance, allowing pretrained text-to-image diffusion models to handle arbitrary aspect ratios and resolutions beyond their training domain. The approach solves the common failure mode of patch-wise editing at non-standard resolutions.

### 📊 Time Series

**[3]** [LLaTiSA: Towards Difficulty-Stratified Time Series Reasoning from Visual Perception to Semantics](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.17295)
LLaTiSA formalizes Time Series Reasoning via a four-level cognitive taxonomy and introduces HiTSR, an 83k-sample dataset with verified Chain-of-Thought trajectories, enabling Vision-Language Models to reason about time series through a curriculum fine-tuning approach. The work establishes rigorous benchmarks for temporal reasoning in multimodal systems.

### 🏃 Motion

**[4]** [Encoder-Free Human Motion Understanding via Structured Motion Descriptions](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.21668)
This work proposes Structured Motion Description (SMD), a rule-based approach converting joint sequences into natural language joint-angle descriptions, enabling LLMs to reason about human motion without learned motion encoders, achieving state-of-the-art on motion QA and captioning benchmarks. The approach is validated across 8 LLMs from 6 families with only lightweight LoRA adaptation.
