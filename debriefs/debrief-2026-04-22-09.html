⚡ Quick Summary

  • Generalization at Edge of Stability: Neural networks trained at large learning rates converge to fractal attractors with low intrinsic dimension, explaining why edge-of-stability training generalizes better — a major theoretical advance.
  • Unified Physical Language for Human-to-Humanoid Transfer: UniT bridges kinematic gaps between humans and humanoids via visual-grounded action tokenization, enabling transfer from egocentric human video.
  • FASTER: Value-Guided Sampling for Fast RL: FASTER traces value gains backward through the diffusion denoising process, achieving test-time scaling benefits without sampling overhead.
  • VLA Foundry: Unified Vision-Language-Action Framework: VLA Foundry open-sources a single training stack for LLM, VLM, and VLA models from language pretraining through action fine-tuning.
  • TEMPO: Test-Time Training That Actually Improves: TEMPO's alternating critic-recalibration pushes Qwen3-14B from 42.3% to 65.8% on AIME 2024, sustaining improvement beyond offline training limits.
  • ClawNet: Cross-User Agent Collaboration Networks: ClawNet introduces human-symbiotic agents where each user owns a permanently bound agent forming governance-secured collaboration networks.

📰 News Headlines

  • Post-quantum cryptography for 5G: Hybrid post-quantum cryptography (HPQC) protocols for 5G-AKA are moving toward standardization, providing forward-secure authentication for quantum-resilient 5G networks.
  • Open-source AI agent governance stack: A 6-library Python governance stack for AI agents (identity binding, scoped authorization, action-level accountability) was open-sourced on HN.
  • LLM agents fail adversarial DAO governance: System 2 reasoning in Qwen-3.5-9B showed 26.7% cognitive collapse rate and 17x latency versus 100%-robust System 1 baseline in adversarial Optimism DAO environment.
  • Chain-of-Thought degrades spatial reasoning: CoT prompting consistently hurts visual spatial task performance across 17 multimodal models, revealing shortcut learning even when images are absent.
  • AI safety x-risk framing criticized: Nature commentary argues existential-risk framing may exclude practitioners and crowd out near-term harm reduction work in AI safety.
