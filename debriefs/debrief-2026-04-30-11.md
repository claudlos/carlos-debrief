# Carlos's Research Debrief — April 30, 2026 11:00

## ⚡ Quick Summary (ArXiv Research Highlights)

- **Diffusion LLMs distilled across architectures**: TIDE enables cross-architecture knowledge transfer for diffusion large language models, compressing 8B/16B teacher models into 0.6B students with 1.53-point average gains and 48.78 HumanEval vs 32.3 baseline.
- **Language diffusion models are associative memories with emergent creativity**: Research demonstrates that Uniform-based Discrete Diffusion Models behave as associative memories — recovering memorized data while generating content beyond stored patterns.
- **HalluCiteChecker fights AI-generated hallucinated citations**: A new toolkit detects and verifies fake citations in scientific papers, addressing a growing threat to scholarly credibility as LLMs become embedded in academic writing.
- **PRAG enables end-to-end privacy-preserving RAG**: A new RAG system achieves full document and query confidentiality without sacrificing retrieval quality, removing the tradeoff between privacy and performance in cloud-augmented LLMs.
- **Speculative decoding accelerates RL post-training 1.8× at 8B scale**: Applied to RL rollouts with vLLM backend, speculative decoding yields 1.8× throughput improvement at 8B; projections suggest 2.5× end-to-end speedup at 235B with async RL.

- **GLM-5V-Turbo: native multimodal agent foundation model**: Integrates perception as a core reasoning component — not a peripheral interface — achieving strong agentic performance across multimodal coding, visual tool use, and framework-based tasks.
- **LLM conversational user simulation survey**: Comprehensive survey covers taxonomy, techniques, and evaluation methods for LLM-based user simulation, consolidating a rapidly growing subfield with applications in dialogue systems.
- **AI terminology confusion undermines scientific discourse**: A critique argues that AI terminology is broadly misused and poorly defined, diluting meaning in ways that complicate research communication and public understanding of AI capabilities.
- **Post-quantum cryptography for IoT authentication**: As quantum computers approach, research explores post-quantum approaches for securing IoT devices and user privacy against future quantum attacks.
- **Quantum machine learning review maps the field**: A comprehensive review covers the integration of AI with quantum computing, from quantum-enhanced classical ML to native quantum algorithms and hybrid approaches.
