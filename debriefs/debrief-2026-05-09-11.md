### 📄 Google Scholar

**Catalyst breakthroughs in methane dry reforming: Employing machine learning for future advancements** [🔗](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0360319924036942)
> Rising levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane (CH4) have sparked interest in converting these greenhouse gases into useful chemicals and fuels via dry reforming. Machine learning is now being employed to accelerate catalyst discovery and optimize reaction conditions for methane dry reforming, offering a promising pathway to environmental remediation and sustainable energy production.

**Advancements in Artificial Intelligence: Breakthroughs, Challenges and the Road Ahead** [🔗](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/11050474/)
> This paper conducts systematic quantitative analysis of AI advancements from 2010 onwards using the Epoch AI notable AI models dataset and literature review. Key findings show industry overtook academia as the dominant source of notable AI models starting in 2014, with deep learning funding spikes aligning with major milestones in 2013, 2016, and 2019.

**Large language models (LLM) in computational social science: prospects, current state, and challenges** [🔗](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13278-025-01428-9)
> This paper surveys how LLMs are being applied in computational social science, identifying key prospects around automated text analysis and social simulation, the current state of adoption, and open challenges around data bias, privacy, and integration with existing methodological toolchains.

**Artificial intelligence and machine learning in cybersecurity: a deep dive into state-of-the-art techniques and future paradigms** [🔗](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10115-025-02429-y)
> This paper provides a comprehensive review of AI and ML techniques applied to cybersecurity, covering adversarial AI, automated threat intelligence, AI-driven security orchestration, and future paradigms for defending networks and systems against AI-enabled attacks.

**Securing the future: exploring post-quantum cryptography for authentication and user privacy in IoT devices** [🔗](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10586-024-04799-4)
> With the emergence of quantum computers, traditional cryptographic methods face existential risk. This survey explores post-quantum cryptographic primitives for IoT authentication and user privacy, reviewing lattice-based, hash-based, and code-based schemes suitable for resource-constrained devices.

**Quantum machine learning: A comprehensive review of integrating AI with quantum computing for computational advancements** [🔗](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2215016125001645)
> This review examines the integration of AI with quantum computing across the full spectrum from quantum-enhanced classical ML to native quantum algorithms, covering applications in optimization, drug discovery, and quantum-secured communications.
