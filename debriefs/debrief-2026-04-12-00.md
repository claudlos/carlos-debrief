## 📄 ArXiv Papers

### 🧠 LLMs

**1. The Master Key Hypothesis: Unlocking Cross-Model Capability Transfer via Linear Subspace Alignment**
*Rishab Balasubramanian, Pin-Jie Lin, Rituraj Sharma, Anjie Fang, Fardin Abdi*

Investigates whether post-trained capabilities can be transferred across models without retraining by aligning capability directions in low-dimensional latent subspaces. Introduces UNLOCK, a training-free framework that extracts capability directions via activation contrast and applies low-rank linear transformations to transfer behaviors like Chain-of-Thought reasoning.

**Why it matters:** Transferring CoT reasoning from Qwen1.5-14B to Qwen1.5-7B yields 12.1% accuracy gain on MATH, and mathematical reasoning transfer from Qwen3-4B-Base to Qwen3-14B-Base improves AGIEval Math from 61.1% to 71.3% — surpassing the post-trained 14B model. This could dramatically reduce training costs by reusing capabilities across model scales.

🔗 [arXiv:2604.06377](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.06377) | [1]

**2. Faithful GRPO: Improving Visual Spatial Reasoning in Multimodal Language Models via Constrained Policy Optimization**
*Sai Srinivas Kancheti, Aditya Kanade, Rohit Sinha, Vineeth N Balasubramanian, Tanuja Ganu*

Studies the problem where RLVR-trained multimodal reasoning models gain accuracy but produce Chain-of-Thought traces that are inconsistent with final answers and poorly grounded in visual evidence. Proposes Faithful GRPO (FGRPO) which enforces consistency and grounding as constraints via Lagrangian dual ascent.

**Why it matters:** Reduces CoT inconsistency rate from 24.5% to 1.7% across seven spatial reasoning datasets on Qwen2.5-VL backbones. Critical for making multimodal reasoning transparent and trustworthy in safety-sensitive applications.

🔗 [arXiv:2604.08476](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.08476) | [2]

**3. ImplicitMemBench: Measuring Unconscious Behavioral Adaptation in Large Language Models**
*Chonghan Qin, Xiachong Feng, Weitao Ma, Xiaocheng Feng, Lingpeng Kong*

Introduces the first benchmark evaluating implicit (non-declarative) memory in LLMs through three cognitive constructs: Procedural Memory, Priming, and Classical Conditioning. Uses a 300-item suite with unified Learning/Priming-Interfere-Test protocol and first-attempt scoring.

**Why it matters:** No model exceeds 66% overall, with dramatic asymmetries (inhibition 17.6% vs. preference 75.0%). Reframes LLM evaluation from "what agents recall" to "what they automatically enact" — revealing universal bottlenecks that require architectural innovations beyond parameter scaling.

🔗 [arXiv:2604.08064](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.08064) | [3]
