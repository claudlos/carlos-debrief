⚡ Quick Summary

  • FutureSim — Grounded world-event simulation where AI agents forecast real news beyond their knowledge cutoff by replaying chronological events, enabling evaluation of adaptive reasoning in open-ended environments.
  • OpenDeepThink — Population-based test-time compute framework using Bradley-Terry pairwise aggregation to scale breadth in LLM reasoning, overcoming the single-trace depth bottleneck without ground-truth verifiers.
  • RefDecoder — Conditional video decoding with equal decoder-side conditioning, addressing detail loss and inconsistency in latent diffusion video generation models.
  • MetaBackdoor — First backdoor attacks on LLMs exploiting positional encoding as the trigger surface, requiring no content modification and bypassing existing defenses.
  • WARD — Adversarially robust prompt injection defense for web agents, addressing generalization to unseen domains and adversarial attacks evolving over time.
  • MeMo (Memory as a Model) — Encodes new domain knowledge into a dedicated memory model without updating LLM parameters, enabling timely specialized information.

📰 News Headlines

  • IoT Fleet Takeover via Cheap Doorbell: A Temu doorbell's sequential device IDs and baked-in forgeable signing strings enable account-wide hijacking — a stark reminder that consumer IoT remains a critical, underregulated attack surface.
  • AI-Discovered CVEs Surge: Public CVE disclosure volumes are rising sharply across major software vendors and open source projects, with evidence increasingly pointing to AI-assisted vulnerability discovery as the primary driver.
  • CTF Scene is Broken: Frontier AI has hollowed out competitive CTF formats, rendering traditional scoreboard performance a weak signal for actual security skill — a challenge the community is still grappling with.
  • ZKPoX Framework Debuts: Orchid Mantis launches as a standalone Zero-Knowledge Proof of Exploit framework, enabling provable demonstration of exploit knowledge without revealing the exploit itself.
  • Post-Quantum IoT Urgent: New research reinforces the pressing need for post-quantum cryptographic standards in IoT devices, as quantum computing threatens to obsolete current encryption before the devices themselves are retired.
