⚡ Quick Summary

  • Computer-use agents are inherently unreliable — even when they succeed once, repeated executions of the same task fail 20–63% of the time, suggesting reliability requires task-redundant scaffolds and repeated evaluation benchmarks (HF, 2604.17849).
  • LLM calibration degrades under on-policy distillation — a Scaling Law of Miscalibration shows that helpful teacher context induces systematic overconfidence; CaOPD proposes calibration-aware OPD to maintain both accuracy and confidence (HF, 2604.16830).
  • LLM reasoning traces can be watermarked to prevent unauthorized distillation — simple instruction-based rewriting degrades student utility while embedding detectable signatures without affecting teacher performance (HF, 2602.15143).
  • LLM agents lack environmental curiosity — they discover task solutions 79-81% of the time but exploit them only 37-50% of the time; available tools and training distribution are key factors (HF, 2604.17609).
  • VoxMind adds agentic tool use to spoken dialogue models — a Multi-Agent Dynamic Tool Management system pushes task completion from 34.88% to 74.57%, outperforming Gemini-2.5-Pro on spoken agent tasks (HF, 2604.15710).
