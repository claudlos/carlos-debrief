⚡ Quick Summary

  • Clinical LLM safety vs. accuracy scaling: A new SaFE-Scale framework reveals that safety and accuracy follow different scaling laws in clinical LLMs — bigger models can be more accurate while being less safe on high-risk, evidence-contradicting errors that matter more than average benchmark scores.
  • AI red teaming in the agentic era: A new framework automates AI red-teaming workflows that previously took weeks, reducing the cycle to hours by dynamically assembling attacks, transforms, and scorers instead of hand-crafting each one.
  • Diffusion-based flow sampling: Flow Sampling introduces a training objective that regresses onto a denoising diffusion drift for sampling from unnormalized densities — enabling efficient data-free generative modeling when the target is defined by a known energy function.
  • Conversational symptom assessment at scale: SymptomAI, deployed via Fitbit, demonstrates end-to-end patient interviewing and differential diagnosis using conversational AI agents, moving beyond curated medical vignettes to everyday real-world symptom reporting.
  • Compositional vulnerability in coding agents: MOSAIC-Bench reveals that AI coding agents can pass per-prompt safety checks yet ship exploitable code when their tasks are decomposed into innocuous-looking engineering tickets — a structural vulnerability in multi-step AI software generation.

📰 News Headlines

  • RIPE NCC SSO token exposure: Over 1,000 third parties were given access to RIPE NCC's all-powerful single sign-on tokens by design — any logged-in user who clicked a single link could unknowingly leak their session to external parties.
  • Cloudflare reads ChatGPT React state on every keystroke: A technical analysis reveals that Cloudflare's infrastructure actively monitors React component state inside ChatGPT's frontend on every user keystroke, raising fresh questions about the boundaries of client-side surveillance in AI products.
  • Predictive video latents boost generation quality: A new Predictive Video VAE (PV-VAE) model achieves 52% faster convergence and a 34.42 FVD improvement by encoding only partial observations and predicting future frames — improving the temporal coherence of video generative models.
  • Autonomous research agents go multi-model adversarial: ARIS, an open-source research harness, coordinates long-horizon ML research by pitting an executor LLM against a reviewer from a different model family by default, catching plausible-but-unsupported claims before they propagate through the research pipeline.
  • Current AI agents average only 47.4% on real workspace tasks: Workspace-Bench, a new benchmark with 388 tasks across 20,476 real files, shows that the best AI agent harness reaches just 68.7% (human baseline: 80.7%), highlighting how far agent systems remain from reliable real-world file-based work.
