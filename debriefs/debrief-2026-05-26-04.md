## ⚡ Quick Summary (ArXiv Research)

- <strong>AI agents remain far from reliable in open-ended tasks:</strong> Claw-Anything shows GPT-5.5 achieves only 34.5% pass@1 on always-on personal assistance; SimuWoB finds SOTA GUI agents at 27.9% on mobile tasks; and CAD agents (GPT-5.5/Claude Code) pass only ~20% of engineering FEA-validated tasks — revealing massive gaps between demos and real-world robustness.
- <strong>Multi-agent and proactive agent architectures advance:</strong> ProAct reduces task turns by 14.8% by anticipating user needs during idle time; SEAL co-evolves agents and training environments for +8-26pt gains with only 400 samples; Foundation Protocol proposes a coordination layer for agent societies with economic primitives and audit trails.
- <strong>Chain-of-thought faithfulness is largely unverifiable:</strong> The BonaFide benchmark (3,066 ground-truth CoTs) shows the best faithfulness metric reaches only 0.70 AUROC, while Zero-CoT Probe reveals LLM reasoning steps actively mask memorization — exposing that CoT auditability tools are unreliable.
- <strong>Diffusion and flow matching reach new efficiency milestones:</strong> RTDMD achieves SOTA on SD3/FLUX.2 with only 4 inference steps by unifying distribution matching with RL; Channel-wise VQ (CVQ) reaches 100% codebook utilization; SOT-CFM constrains flow matching on a hypersphere manifold for superior image generation.
- <strong>3D/video generation matures toward simulation-ready assets:</strong> TriSplat outputs simulation-ready meshes directly from a forward pass; Pantheon360 enables 360° video generation with a 3D Cache scaffold; Helix4D handles complex 4D topology changes; and WBench provides the first comprehensive benchmark for interactive world models.

### Agents (22)

**QUEST: Training Frontier Deep Research Agents with Fully Synthetic Tasks** [[1]](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.24218)
*Authors: Jian Xie, Tianhe Lin, Zilu Wang, Yuting Ning, Yuekun Yao*

QUEST is a family of open deep research agents (2B to 35B) trained on 8K synthesized tasks using unified rubric trees, approaching or surpassing frontier closed-source agents across eight deep research benchmarks and achieving best overall performance among open-weight agents.

Tags: cs.AI, cs.CL

**Decoding the Critique Mechanism in Large Reasoning Models** [[2]](https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.16331)
*Authors: Hoang Phan, Quang H. Nguyen, Hung T. Q. Le, Xiusi Chen, Heng Ji*

Large Reasoning Models use a 'hidden critique ability' — an internal mechanism that detects errors and triggers self-correction even when the chain-of-thought doesn't verbalize corrections — and steering latent representations with a critique vector improves error detection.

Tags: cs.CL, cs.AI

Foundation Protocol (FP) is a graph-first coordination layer for an emerging human-AI society that unifies agents, tools, resources, humans, and organizations, providing economic primitives for metering and settlement while treating policy, provenance, and audit as first-class concerns.

Tags: cs.AI, cs.MA

Tags: cs.AI, cs.HC

**Faithfulness Metrics Don't Measure Faithfulness: A Meta-Evaluation with Ground Truth** [[5]](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.25052)
*Authors: Yoav Gur-Arieh, Ana Marasović, Mor Geva*

BonaFide, a benchmark of 3,066 ground-truth labeled CoTs, reveals that most faithfulness metrics for chain-of-thought reasoning perform near chance, with the best reaching only 0.70 AUROC at CoT level — indicating current CoT auditability tools are largely unreliable.

Tags: cs.AI, cs.CL

**The Illusion of Reasoning: Exposing Evasive Data Contamination in LLMs via Zero-CoT Truncation** [[6]](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.21856)
*Authors: Yifan Lan, Yuanpu Cao, Hanyu Wang, Lu Lin, Jinghui Chen*

Zero-CoT Probe (ZCP) truncates chain-of-thought to expose latent shortcut mappings and detect evasive data contamination in LLMs, introducing Contamination Confidence to quantify both likelihood and severity of contamination beyond binary classification.

Tags: cs.CL, cs.AI

**Claw-Anything: Benchmarking Always-On Personal Assistants** [[7]](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.26086)

Claw-Anything benchmarks always-on personal assistants with months of simulated user activity, finding GPT-5.5 achieves only 34.5% pass@1, substantially below prior benchmarks and revealing a significant gap between current agent capabilities and always-on assistance demands.

Tags: cs.AI, cs.HC

**ProAct: Anticipate and Learn — Unleashing Idle-Time Compute in Proactive Agents** [[8]](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.25971)
*Authors: Haoyi Hu, Qirong Lyu, Xianghan Kong, Weiwen Liu, Jianghao Lin*

ProAct is a proactive agent architecture that leverages idle-time compute between user interactions to anticipate and prepare for likely upcoming needs, reducing task completion turns by 14.8% and hallucination rates by 28.1%.

Tags: cs.AI, cs.CL

**SEAL: Synergistic Co-Evolution of Agents and Learning Environments** [[9]](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.24426)

SEAL co-evolves both the agent policy and its training environment in a closed loop, using turn-level failure diagnoses as a shared signal for both environment adaptation and policy optimization, yielding +8.25 to +26.25 average-point gains with only 400 training samples.
